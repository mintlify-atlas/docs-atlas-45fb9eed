The Conversation History panel records every exchange in your session. It auto-scrolls to the latest message and gives you a permanent record to study after the conversation ends.

## What's recorded

Each entry in the history is a `ConversationTurn`:

```typescript types.ts
export interface ConversationTurn {
  id: string;             // Unique ID for the turn
  role: 'user' | 'ai';   // Who spoke
  text: string;           // Hanzi for AI, recognized speech for User
  pinyin?: string;        // Pinyin (AI turns only)
  translation?: string;   // English translation (AI turns only)
  timestamp: number;      // Unix timestamp (ms)
}
```

### AI turns

AI messages display three lines:
- **Hanzi** — the Chinese characters in bold
- **Pinyin** — romanized pronunciation in monospace
- **Translation** — English translation in italics

This mirrors the triple-layered subtitle format so you can cross-reference what was said.

### User turns

User messages display the recognized Mandarin text that the AI transcribed from your speech. This is useful for checking whether the AI heard you correctly.

## Using the history for study

<Steps>
  <Step title="Review vocabulary after the session">
    Scroll through the AI turns to collect new words. The Pinyin column makes it easy to check pronunciation without a separate dictionary.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Check your own accuracy">
    Look at your turns to see what the AI transcribed. If it misheard you, that's a signal to work on that particular sound or tone.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Repeat with a lesson">
    Run the same lesson again with the history visible. Compare what you said this time versus last time to track progress.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Layout and visibility

The History panel occupies the left column of the desktop layout (`lg:col-span-1`). It is hidden on mobile and tablet screens to conserve space for the avatar and subtitle display.

<Note>
  The history panel is only visible on large screens (1024px and above). On mobile, review your conversation by scrolling back through the subtitle area or noting the pronunciation feedback cards.
</Note>

## History lifecycle

- History is **cleared** when you start a new conversation (press **Start Conversation**)
- History is **not persisted** between browser sessions — refreshing the page wipes it
- There is no export function — copy text manually if you want to save a session

<Tip>
  Keep a vocabulary notebook open beside the app. After each session, copy any new words from the history into your notes along with their Pinyin and translation.
</Tip>
